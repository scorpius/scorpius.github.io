Eliminate JPEG Artifacts
March 5, 2000

Sampling
To eliminate jpeg artifacts, set the horizontal and vertical component sampling factors to 1x1. Sadly, Blender and most other jpeg encoders default to 2x1 and do not allow it to be changed. This little known trick yields the highest possible quality for jpeg, with a very slight increase in file size.


Quality Factor
Never use a quality factor higher than 95, it usually inflates the file size and makes no visible difference. A quality factor of 100 combined with 2x1 sampling will produce more artifacts than a quality factor of 95 with 1x1 sampling.

This technique, with emphasis on the sampling factors, should greatly reduce banding that occurs in areas of gradients and other blocky-atifacts that appear near edges and areas of high contrast. It also works wonders for screen grabs of dialog boxes and for delicate line art. However, as rendered images become more and more realistic (due to radiosity & global illumination, procedural textures that replicate natural patterns, etc.) the 1x1 sampling trick doesn't have as much effect as it used to. But it will always have its uses.


CJPEG users can adjust the sampling with this command:
cjpeg -progressive -opt -qual 95 -sample 1x1 name.tga name.jpg
